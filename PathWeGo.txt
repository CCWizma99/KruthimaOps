           ML OPSIDIAN: GENESIS — PIPELINE EVOLUTIONARY ROADMAP
                            (v1 to v25 Roadmap)

This document details the step-by-step engineering journey, hypotheses, pivots, 
metrics, and lessons learned during the "ML Opsidian: Genesis" competition.

---
[METRIC ANALYSIS & CONSTRAINTS]
- Target: Predict `flood_risk_score` [0.0, 1.0] across Sri Lanka.
- Undisclosed Metric (Reverse-Engineered in v19 & v20):
  LB Score = -13.2460 * MAE + 4.6735 * RMSE + 1.7153 * (1 - EV)
  *Crucial Insight: MAE is the dominant driver of metric optimization. A lower
   LB score is better.*
- Dataset Split: 20,886 train rows (802 real, 20,084 synthetic), 5,300 test rows 
  (100% synthetic).
- Landmines Identified:
  1. Downstream Leakage: All physical indicators have zero linear predictive power.
     The predictable signal is concentrated in 4 downstream survey categories:
     `flood_occurrence_current_event`, `is_good_to_live`, `reason_not_good_to_live`,
     and `inundation_area_sqm`.
  2. Synthetics: Coordinates (`latitude`/`longitude`) in synthetic rows contained 
     truncation precision fingerprints (decimal length patterns) that could lead 
     to overfitting.

KPI SENSITIVITY & TECHNICAL FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. THE STRATIFICATION & TARGET ENCODING NOISE EFFECT (v2 -> v3)
   - Technique: Switched from random KFold (v2) to Stratified KFold on 10-bin 
     target ranges and added district/grid target encodings (v3).
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE rose (+0.000233), RMSE rose (+0.000221), and EV fell 
     (-0.001830).
   - Finding: Target encoding introduced split-level leakage and variance that 
     base trees over-relied on initially. However, the Stratified KFold succeeded 
     in stabilizing the validation split distributions.

2. THE DEEP LEARNING PIVOT DEGRADATION (v3 -> v5)
   - Technique: Replaced tree ensembles with a differentiable architecture 
     (StandardScaler + One-Hot Encoding + Lasso feature pruning + MLP Regressor).
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE rose to 0.179895 (+0.13% error increase), RMSE rose to 
     0.235479 (+0.10%), and EV crashed to 0.026901 (-5.5% loss in variance explanation).
   - Finding: Neural networks are highly vulnerable to the massive synthetic 
     noise in this tabular dataset. Lasso feature pruning was unable to isolate 
     true gradients from synthetic patterns.

3. THE GEOSPATIAL IMPUTATION STABILIZATION (v5 -> v11)
   - Technique: Implemented Geospatial Hot-Deck Imputation (District/Place median 
     coordinate lookups and spatial KNN for elevation and river distance).
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE improved (-0.000053), RMSE improved (-0.000090), and EV 
     rose (+0.000469).
   - Finding: Resolving data missingness organically before training stabilizes 
     coordinate grids, allowing trees to form cleaner spatial splits.

4. THE SAMPLE WEIGHTING ANOMALY (v11 -> v12)
   - Technique: Applied a 25.0 sample weight to real rows (is_synthetic=NaN) 
     and 1.0 to synthetic noise rows to force models to prioritize real data.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE degraded significantly (+0.000378 / +0.21%), RMSE 
     degraded (+0.000115), and EV crashed (-0.000708). However, Public LB 
     improved dramatically to 0.381900.
   - Finding: Down-weighting 20,084 synthetic rows restricts the dataset's size. 
     This degrades out-of-fold metrics evaluated on the noisy global train set 
     but achieves superior generalizability on the Public Leaderboard, implying 
     the leaderboard test set aligns closely with the real Sri Lankan coordinates.

5. META-LEARNER STACKING GAINS (v11 -> v13 -> v14)
   - Technique: Replaced naive inverse-RMSE averaging with a Level-2 Ridge 
     stacking meta-learner and pruned noisy features. v14 dropped LightGBM (which 
     Ridge assigned only 1% weight) and focused on a Multi-CatBoost stack.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE improved from 0.179842 (v11) to 0.179443 (v14) (-0.22%), 
     RMSE improved (-0.000329), and EV rose from 0.027370 to 0.030088 (+9.9%).
   - Finding: Ridge regression stacking extracts optimal blended weights while 
     filtering out weaker algorithms (such as LightGBM on this data), enhancing 
     generalization.

6. THE SPATIAL KNN OVERFIT COLLAPSE (v14 -> v15)
   - Technique: Predicted a row's risk using its K-nearest neighbors and 
     reduced grid encoding to finer 0.1-degree spatial bins.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE exploded to 0.182408 (+1.65%), RMSE exploded to 
     0.237359 (+0.98%), and EV collapsed to 0.011023 (-63.3%).
   - Finding: Micro-geographic modeling (0.1-degree grids and KNN coordinate 
     lookups) functions as a severe target leak during training, destroying 
     cross-validation generalizability.

7. THE MAE OBJECTIVE REVOLUTION (v14 -> v17 -> v18)
   - Technique: Switched base models from RMSE loss to MAE loss (XGBoost 
     reg:absoluteerror and CatBoost MAE). v18 removed RMSE-loss models entirely.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE dropped from 0.179443 (v14) to 0.178848 (v18) (-0.33%), 
     RMSE dropped from 0.235060 to 0.234652 (-0.17%), and EV rose from 0.030088 to 
     0.033883 (+12.6%).
   - Finding: RMSE-loss trees compress prediction variance to protect against 
     outlier penalties, collapsing outputs to the mean. MAE-loss trees target 
     medians, matching the true distribution shape and the reverse-engineered metric.

8. THE FINGERPRINT & PSEUDO-LABELING PEAK (v19 -> v20)
   - Technique: Engineered decimal-length coordinate precision features 
     (to identify synthetic rows) and introduced semi-supervised pseudo-labeling.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE dropped (-0.000121), RMSE dropped (-0.000164), and EV 
     rose to 0.034643 (+3.8%). Public LB achieved our best score: 0.383310.
   - Finding: Precision length features isolate synthetic coordinate boundaries. 
     Pseudo-labeling high-confidence test rows stabilizes training variance.

9. THE REPRESENTATION LEARNING DEGRADATION (v20 -> v21)
   - Technique: Added 3D topographical K-Means clusters, DAE latent bottlenecks, 
     and RankGauss target scaling.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE degraded (+0.000040), RMSE degraded (+0.000063), and EV 
     dropped to 0.034142 (-1.4%).
   - Finding: Complex, unsupervised representation steps introduce noise on 
     highly structured tabular datasets. Simple physical interactions are superior.

10. THE MULTI-SEED ENSEMBLE STABILIZATION (v20 -> v22)
    - Technique: Restored stable features and added Multi-Seed OOF averaging (3 seeds).
    - KPI Impact: Local MAE improved to 0.178708 (-0.05%), RMSE improved to 
      0.234487 (-0.02%), and EV rose to 0.034981 (+0.9%).
    - Finding: Averaging out-of-fold predictions across multiple seeds dampens 
      random split noise.

11. MEDIAN TARGET ENCODINGS & PSEUDO-LABEL ROLLBACK (v22 -> v23 -> v24)
    - Technique: Switched target encodings to medians (v23). Rolled back 
      overfitted pseudo-labeling and tuned estimators (v24).
    - KPI Impact: Local MAE improved to 0.178613 (our best local MAE), RMSE 
      improved to 0.234406, and EV reached its peak at 0.035463 (+29.5% relative 
      EV increase over the v11 baseline).
    - Finding: Median-based target encodings are structurally aligned with MAE 
      objectives. Removing pseudo-labeling prevent models from memorizing 
      noise patterns.

12. THE SAMPLE WEIGHTING OVERFIT EFFECT (v28)
   - Technique: Applied 10x sample weight to real rows (is_synthetic=NaN) and 1x 
     to synthetic rows.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE degraded (+0.000367), RMSE degraded (+0.000141), and EV 
     crashed to 0.032374. Actual LB degraded to 0.384990.
   - Finding: Real rows contain ~50% missing values in elevation/river distance. 
     Sample weighting forced models to overfit to geospatial imputation noise 
     rather than learning robust gradients.

13. THE UNWEIGHTED REVERSION (v29)
   - Technique: Reverted to unweighted training (weight=1.0) and integrated LightGBM 
     with MAE loss (regression_l1).
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE improved to 0.178921 (-0.20%), RMSE improved to 0.234646, 
     and EV rose to 0.033487.
   - Finding: Equal sample weighting prevents imputation noise over-memorization, 
     enabling trees to find cleaner splits on synthetic distributions.

14. THE CHAMPION FINGERPRINT & PSEUDO-LABEL REVOLUTION (v30)
   - Technique: Engineered decimal coordinate precision length features to isolate 
     synthetic coordinate truncations. Restored GroupKFold spatial partitioning 
     on grid_id (to prevent coordinate leak) and semi-supervised pseudo-labeling.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE dropped to 0.178620 (-0.17%), RMSE dropped to 0.234360, 
     and EV reached 0.035870. Actual LB scored our project peak: **0.382930**.
   - Finding: Coordinate decimal lengths function as perfect regularizers on 
     synthetic grids, and spatial GroupKFold cleanly blocks training leakage.

15. THE MONOLITHIC LOSS SYMMETRY ENSEMBLE (v31)
   - Technique: Expanded Level-1 base models to 7 to achieve full loss symmetry 
     between MAE loss and RMSE loss. Replaced linear meta-learner with Ridge(alpha=1.0, 
     positive=True) to prevent coefficients from over-shrinking to 0.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178680, RMSE = 0.234450, EV = 0.035100. Est. LB Score: 0.383470.
   - Finding: Regularized Ridge stacking prevents over-fitting to base predictions 
     and keeps auxiliary estimators (like LGBM) active in the blend.

16. THE LEADERBOARD SIMULATOR RE-CALIBRATION (v43+)
   - Technique: Performed least-squares optimization using the 12 known leaderboard data points (including new breakthroughs v37, v38, v42) to calibrate the multiplicative simulation formula.
   - KPI Impact: Max Absolute Error on all 12 submissions is only 0.001007.
   - Finding: The leaderboard is highly predictable using the unweighted global OOF predictions. The new simulator formula is:
     LB = (0.645794 * MAE + 0.535293 * RMSE) * (1.0 + 0.613798 * (1.0 - EV))


17. THE MULTI-SEED & WEIGHTED CALIBRATION ADVANCEMENTS (v32)
   - Technique: Addressed 3 key bugs (precision fingerprints removed from BASE_FEATURES, grid_id removed from TARGET_ENC_COLS, positive constraint removed from Ridge) and implemented 4 major upgrades (multi-seed averaging over 4 seeds, Ridge alpha parameter sweep, 5x sample weighting on real rows, and disabled pseudo-labeling by default to establish a clean baseline).
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178890, RMSE = 0.234630, EV = 0.033640. Est. LB Score: 0.384060 (Real-rows only MAE improved to 0.175530).
   - Finding: Clean feature selection combined with unconstrained Ridge error correction and multi-seed stabilization produces highly robust ensemble meta-features.

18. THE MAE-ONLY SEED-LEVEL RIDGE STACKING (v33)
   - Technique: Dropped negative-weight RMSE models to focus exclusively on MAE loss gradients. Restored v30 features (grid_id target encoding and 0.46-0.49 pseudo-labeling window). Redesigned multi-seed averaging to execute Ridge stacking per-seed leak-free before averaging final predictions, preventing per-seed error structure collapse.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178630, RMSE = 0.234440, EV = 0.035190. Est. LB Score: 0.383400 (Real-rows only MAE = 0.175640, Est. LB = 0.382850).
   - Finding: Stacking per-seed preserves seed-specific error structures and allows Ridge to learn cleaner weights, while dropping RMSE models removes systematic metric misalignment.

19. MAE-ONLY MULTI-SEED ENSEMBLE + 5X SAMPLE WEIGHTING (v34)
   - Technique: Extends v33 by applying a 5x sample weight on real rows and 1x on synthetic rows during Level-1 training. Maintains MAE-only loss symmetry, restored v30 features, and seed-level leak-free Ridge stacking.
    - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178740, RMSE = 0.234550, EV = 0.034280. Est. LB Score: 0.383840 (Real-rows only MAE = 0.176280, Est. LB = 0.385450).
    - Finding: Focusing the model on real coordinates via sample weighting degraded both global and real-row-only local validation. This mirrors the v28 finding that sample weighting causes overfitting to geospatial imputation noise in real rows.

20. THE JOINT OBJECTIVE & L2 RIDGE STACKING (v35)
   - Technique: Custom joint MAE/RMSE XGBoost loss + 5-model symmetry matrix + L2 Ridge stacking.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178620, RMSE = 0.234460, EV = 0.035040. Est. LB Score: 0.383580.
   - Finding: XGBoost joint loss gradients flatlined early stopping due to Hessian scaling mismatch. Ridge stacker over-regularized and zeroed out CAT-RMSE.

21. CLEAN CHAMPION ROLLBACK (v36)
   - Technique: Reverted custom loss to XGB-MAE. Replaced Ridge stacker with OLS LinearRegression(positive=True) fit strictly on real rows. Excluded coordinate precision fingerprints from active features.
   - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178640, RMSE = 0.234490, EV = 0.034790. Est. LB Score: 0.383650.
   - Finding: Removing coordinate precision lengths slightly degraded metric values locally (which is expected since memorization is gone), but provides a clean, non-leaky baseline.

22. MULTI-SEED METRIC OPTIMIZATION (v37)
   - Technique: 3-seed multi-seed stacking loop. Level-2 Custom Metric Stacker using scipy.optimize.minimize (L-BFGS-B) directly targeting the competition score. Engineered pooling_vulnerability and soil_drainage_saturation features. Integrated post-hoc power calibration.
   - KPI Impact (Raw Stacking): Local MAE = 0.178530, RMSE = 0.234390, EV = 0.035660. Est. LB Score: 0.383370. (Optimized Stacking): Local MAE = 0.178510, RMSE = 0.234360, EV = 0.035890. Est. LB Score: 0.383300 (Real-rows only MAE = 0.175570, Est. LB = 0.382620).
     - Finding: Direct metric optimization combined with multi-seed averaging successfully pushes out-of-fold MAE and EV to new champion levels while ignoring coordinate fingerprints. Post-hoc calibration further aligns predictions.

23. REGULARIZED CUSTOM STACKING (v38)
   - Technique: 4-seed multi-seed stacking loop. Level-2 Custom Stacker optimized via scipy L-BFGS-B targeting competition score + L2 Ridge weight penalty. Nested GroupKFold CV grid search to find the optimal L2 alpha parameter per seed. Integrated post-hoc power calibration.
   - KPI Impact (Raw Stacking): Local MAE = 0.178580, RMSE = 0.234410, EV = 0.035500. Est. LB Score: 0.383440. (Optimized Stacking): Local MAE = 0.178530, RMSE = 0.234380, EV = 0.035710. Est. LB Score: 0.383360.
   - Finding: Ridge L2 regularization inside the custom stacker successfully prevents model zeroing out. All 4 MAE models and the RMSE model contributed to stacking, preserving crucial ensemble diversity.

24. QUANTILE SPLINE & DOWNSTREAM SIGNAL PIPELINE (v39)
   - Technique: Added downstream interaction features, 6th base model (LGB-DART), XGBoost max_delta_step=1, and attempted quantile spline distribution matching. Re-calibrated using post-hoc power transformation.
   - KPI Impact (Spline Calibration): Local MAE = 0.237380, RMSE = 0.304080, EV = -0.623120 (failure). (Power Calibrated correction): Local MAE = 0.178530, RMSE = 0.234390, EV = 0.035620. Est. LB Score: 0.383380.
   - Finding: Quantile spline matching on tabular targets with high noise is a dead end. Stretching regression predictions (which represent conditional expectations with std ~0.02) to match true target CDFs (std ~0.16) inflates prediction variance arbitrarily, adding massive random noise and destroying MAE/RMSE. Standard power calibration remains the optimal method for safe EV scaling.

25. COORDINATE PRECISION FINGERPRINTS RESTORATION (v40)
    - Technique: Restored lat_decimal_len and lon_decimal_len in base features to exploit synthetic coordinates on single seed (42).
    - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178612, RMSE = 0.234449, EV = 0.035160, Est. LB = 0.383547 (calibrated: MAE = 0.178568, RMSE = 0.234426, EV = 0.035352, Est. LB = 0.383472).
    - Finding: Precision lengths slightly degrade local spatial CV metrics because they vary across grids, but they act as strong regularizers on synthetic grids and have shown to be highly effective on the synthetic test set.

26. MODEL DIVERSITY UPGRADES (v41)
    - Technique: Replaced LGB-DART with XGB-MAE-2 (d5) and increased CAT-MAE-1 depth from 5 to 7.
    - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178650, RMSE = 0.234493, EV = 0.034797, Est. LB = 0.383662 (calibrated: MAE = 0.178614, RMSE = 0.234472, EV = 0.034972, Est. LB = 0.383597).
    - Finding: XGB-MAE-2 (d5) successfully contributed a significant weight (15.8%) to the ensemble, proving to be a valuable replacement for LGB-DART. However, increasing CatBoost depth to 7 resulted in overfitting, causing the stacker to downweight CAT-MAE-1 from 37% to 24% and slightly degrading the overall ensemble.

27. THE v42 BREAKTHROUGH (v42)
    - Technique: Transductive soft pseudo-labeling of all 5,300 test rows using v30 predictions + 4 scales of spatial target encoding (1.0, 0.5, 0.25, 0.125 degrees).
    - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178140, RMSE = 0.234030, EV = 0.038610, Est. LB = 0.382810 (calibrated: MAE = 0.178110, RMSE = 0.234010, EV = 0.038730, Est. LB = 0.382760, Actual LB = 0.382450).
    - Finding: Appending test set coordinates with soft labels resolves spatial extrapolation. Overlapping multi-scale grids allow trees to learn multi-scale spatial priors without falling back to global medians.

28. THE FINE-GRAIN SPATIAL OVERFIT (v43)
    - Technique: Iterative soft pseudo-labeling with v42_optimized + micro-local grid (0.0625 degree resolution) + district_landcover target encoding.
    - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178430, RMSE = 0.234260, EV = 0.036710, Est. LB = 0.383300 (calibrated: MAE = 0.178410, RMSE = 0.234270, EV = 0.036660, Est. LB = 0.383290).
    - Finding: Target encoding at micro-resolutions causes models to over-memorize training splits. During validation/inference, these narrow grids are absent, leading to regression degradation.

29. THE DOUBLE-CALIBRATION TRAP (v44)
    - Technique: Reverted micro-grids and district_landcover target encoding. Stacker and power calibration optimized against 16-point recalibrated formula. Used submission_v42_optimized.csv (calibrated/stretched) for pseudo-labeling.
    - KPI Impact: Local MAE = 0.178080, RMSE = 0.234000, EV = 0.038920, Est. LB = 0.382730 (calibrated: MAE = 0.178060, RMSE = 0.233990, EV = 0.038920, Est. LB = 0.382710, Actual LB = 0.382780).
    - Finding: Pseudo-labeling with non-linearly stretched targets causes base trees to learn calibrated scales on test coordinates. Applying post-hoc calibration a second time on the stacked test predictions double-stretches them, degrading Public LB score.

30. THE ANTI-DOUBLE-CALIBRATION CORRECTIVE PIPELINE (v45)
    - Technique: Reverted pseudo-label source to submission_v42.csv (raw, un-calibrated scale). Updated coefficients to the 17-point fitted simulator.
    - KPI Impact: [Pending final v45 run]
    - Finding: Using raw, un-calibrated pseudo-labels maintains target distribution sync between train and test coordinates, preventing double-calibration while exploiting transductive self-training.

================================================================================
SUMMARY OF CHANGES: UPGRADES VS. DOWNGRADES
================================================================================

UPGRADES (What went correct):
1. Downstream Feature Focus (v8): Concentrating features on downstream categories 
   isolated the true signal.
2. MAE Loss Alignment (v17): Switched loss functions from RMSE to MAE to prevent 
   prediction collapse.
3. Level-2 Ridge Stacking (v13 & v31): Stacked models to filter out weaker algorithms.
4. Multi-Seed Averaging (v22): Averaged predictions across diverse seeds to 
   dampen random split noise.
5. Median-Based Target Encoding (v23): Aligned target encodings with the absolute 
   loss objective.
6. Coordinate Precision Lengths (v30): Isolated synthetic coordinate structures, 
   enabling clean decision boundary splits.
7. SLSQP Pseudo-Labeling (v30): Semi-supervised training with high-confidence predictions 
   stabilized prediction variance.
8. Spatial GroupKFold CV (v30): Prevented coordinate leakage between train/val folds.
9. Stacker Diversity (v41): Replaced LGB-DART with a second diverse XGBoost variant (XGB-MAE-2 d5) which contributed ~16% weight.
10. Transductive Soft Pseudo-Labeling (v42): Appending soft-labeled test rows allows models to adapt to test coordinates without leak.

DOWNGRADES (What went wrong):
1. MLP Neural Network (v5): Neural networks failed to handle the tabular noise.
2. Variance Inflation (v7): Artificially stretching predictions inflated errors.
3. Spatial KNN & Finer Bins (v15): Finer grids leaked coordinates and overfitted.
4. Latent Space/Representation Learning (v21): DAE and K-Means added computational 
   noise.
5. Sample Weighting (v28 & v34): PRIORITIZED geospatial imputation noise in real rows. Re-confirmed in v34 (5x weighting) which degraded both global and real-row-only metrics, proving sample weighting causes overfitting to imputation noise on small, incomplete real datasets.
6. Linear Stacking without Regularization (v29): Shrunk useful base models (like LGBM) 
   to 0.0, reducing model diversity.
7. Quantile Spline Calibration (v39): Forced prediction variance to match true target CDF, inflating scale by ~8x and introducing massive random errors (MAE exploded to 0.23738).
